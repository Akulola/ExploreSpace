The James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope is a large, infrared-optimized space observatory. It was launched on December 25, 2021, and commissioned in January 2022. The telescope is designed to observe some of the most distant objects in the universe, including the formation of the first galaxies, stars, and planetary systems.

Fun facts about the James Webb Space Telescope is that it's named after James E. Webb, a prominent figure in NASA's history who served as the agency's administrator during the 1960s. The telescope's sunshield is about the size of a tennis court, and its mirrors are made of ultra-lightweight beryllium. Additionally, the James Webb Space Telescope is one of the most complex and ambitious space observatories ever built, with a mission lifespan of at least 10 years.
